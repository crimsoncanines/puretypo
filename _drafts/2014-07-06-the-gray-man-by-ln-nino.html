I picked up this book after reading the summary from a book reviewing site. I wanted to read something well spun and this book seemed to fit the bill. And the fact that this is very short gave me a confidence that I could finish it.

At the end of the book I have quite mixed feeling about the book. Here is my opinion.

"But we feared, nevertheless, for the only thing lovers fear the more than being set apart from each other is being forced to be together in the world not worthy of their love."


Cover-page:  the copy I received did not really have a cover page. So I had to go to goodreads to check what the book looked like. There is a gray man sitting wearing orange. It is simple and thought provoking. It does complete justice to the story and the language. Well done.

Characters: the characters in the book are not too many. There is narrator himself who is talking about himself and his muse Carla. There is also a gray man in the book who does not come into picture until the end of the book. Every character has its own language. The way the author has described them in the book is impeccable.

Story line: the story is about the narrator and his love Carla, who live in not so well developed country. Both of them want something more out of their lives but the narrator is too timid to own up to it. Carla convinces him to move out of their country to the big city where he could have his own book published and they could have a fine life for themselves. The narrator reluctantly agrees and they set off to their new journey.

" To love is to want to impose the object of love on the whole world."


Concept: The book is about the self discovery of the narrator or rather him surrendering to the pressure of the big city and forming intimacy with himself. He finally recognizes the struggle of publishing his own book in the big city, written in a language which is not his native and losing himself to the black hole of existence.


Language: the book is a psycho narration. The writing style of the author is very interesting. Sometimes the language is simple and straight forward and the next moment is a whirlwind, which resonates with the narrator of the book.

"What i saw, however, was the exact opposite of it, the fulfillment of my worst fears - the depth of literature reduced to the shallowness of entertainment. Nay, it was worse than my fears - it was Beauty eaten down to its bones and excreted as Fun." 


Good points: good points include the plot, the concept, language and I must say the length of the book.

Bad points: I still find it difficult to come in terms with the sudden changes in the book. There is one thing leading to another in most of the books but this one is full of hairpin curves. It’s like you are hit by a yellow school bus and you realize you are an alien.

Overall: overall I find the book very amusing. The author is definitely someone to watch out for. I went on looking for other books he has written and could find only one. “The Brain within Its Groove” seems another interesting book. Will be back with the review of this book.
