”Midnight’s Children“ is the book that got me reading contemporary fiction. Salman Rushdie has a way with words. With different characters playing together and the voices talking like they are in your head, this author left me mesmerized. So when I watched the trailer of the movie of the same name I had to watch it!  


The book: Midnight’s children is a book about a boy who is born at the midnight of the Independence Day. This has given him a virtue. That could be the only constant companion of his in the entire book. Saleem Sinai can have a telepathic conversation with all the children born at the stroke of midnight.

The book also traces the history of India, right before the partition to the indo-pak war. Saleem Sinai finds himself to be the part of the history as history is part of him. The book begins with describing the how his grandfather met his grandmother and the lineage.

The book leaves you mesmerized. The writing is much spoken about. This book will tell you why Salman Rushdie is the king of contemporary fiction!   

The movie: the movie is directed by Meera Nair. She is a director with a vision. She makes her characters come alive on Celluloid. I must say Salman Rushdie and Meera Nair is the duo who compelled me to watch the movie. But sadly the movie is not as well done as the others by the same director. It maybe because of the complex characters and difficult to imagine situations that the movie could not do justice to the book.

The movie has a larger than life star cast. Everyone has done a good job with acting, but the problem comes when the movie is stitched together. One can identify the gaping holes in the movie. There is also no rhythm in the story. One may find oneself looking at the exit door right in the middle of the movie.  
  
In exile, I learned about power.

Who wins?:  I would definitely say that the movie does not do justice to the book that won the author booker prize. I would any day pick up the book to read all over again. Sadly the same thing cannot be said about the movie.
