There are some ghosts that never leave you. And seldom do those who follow you from childhood. This book is a tale of a women who struggled in her life throughout to come up in her life. I have huge respect for such people. I almost overlooked this book assuming it to be a fiction as you may feel by the cover. Only at the second glance it interested me enough to read. So here is what I feel about the book.


 
We live and die by the choices we make, whether good or bad.
 
 
Cover-page:  cover page is a huge disappointment. It looks good as a composition but does little to tell you what genre it is from. It does not look like a memoir at all. Someone like me who is interested in reading true stories will probably give this one a pass. Not impressed at all.

 

Characters:  the author narrates this book in first person. She talks about her life and few people who made it worse. Basically it is the author all over the book.  

 

After the first incident, I stayed with him for five years before separating. why five years? Because you don't think it's abuse. You think everything but that; you make excuses to why it's happening and your emotions are all over the place because this is someone you love.... and if you've seen this behaviour as a child, it is a familiar place and it is a norm.

 

 

Story line: it is a memoir of the author who is born to a dysfunctional family. Dad was a drunkard, wife beater, drug addict and gambler.  Mom never had the courage to leave him and start over. Roch decides that she wants something better out of life but she keeps tumbling down every time. Fallen into bad friend circle, the men in her life do not help her much. The story that the author has to tell is indeed very interesting. The good thing about the book is that it does not end on a sad note. She tells you how she has forgiven and moved on. The courage of the author is truly inspiring.  

 

Concept: the concept is not very different but very interesting. The author has chosen to tell her story and she has held no bar. I am sure a lot of people who read non-fiction are going to like this.

 

Language: this is the saddest section of the book. the story is told in first person. the author has written this book as how she speaks. English may not be her first language, therefore there are a lot of grammatical errors. there is also a constant reference of "cheque" as "check". it happens a lot with self published authors. they are not always equipped with best language skills. therefore it is best to run it through an editor.
 

The black rose symbolizes pain, hurt, sorrow and death.... a physical death and, or, spiritual death. It also symbolizes strength, courage and resistance... all of which are excerpts of my life.

 

Good points: the good point about the book is that it is not sad at all. it is inspiring. a lot of people who are dealing with personal issues are going to like this book.

 

Bad points: the cover could have been definitely better. the language of course is a major put off. a bad English is always the sore to a reader, even though it is from an author whose first language is not English.  

 

Overall: the book is definitely a good read. there are some major concerns but all in all it is a good read for anyone who is dealing with a troubled life and needs some inspiration.
