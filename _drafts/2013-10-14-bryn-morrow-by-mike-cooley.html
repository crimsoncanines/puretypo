ebook, 80 pages
Published September 10th 2013

I received a review copy of Bryn Morrow from story cartel. I wanted to read it since a very long time not because I felt drawn towards it but for a simple reason that I wanted to try a genre that I do not read much. This book falls into the fantasy genre and somehow I am glad that I have kept myself away from this category. The book is a complete disaster and let me tell you how! And I am being as honest as I could be!


Cover-page: cover-page of the book seems as if it has been taken out from a discarded graphic novel. It is a failed attempt to describe the inside story f the book, sadly. The character on the cover is Bryn. He not only looks very animated but also fails to connect with the reader in the first glance or subsequent ones for that matter. 

Characters: characters in the book are, wait, let me recall. 
Hmm... So there is Bryn Morrow who everybody thinks is a vampire but really is not. He is just a latent. At least that is what he claims. 
Bryn is in search of this girl called Celesta. She is the girl who has crazy dreams and recognises Bryn from her dreams. 
And, of course, the villain. Simon is also a vampire, opppsss, latent. Or it’s okay. Not that he knows it himself. 

Concept: for once I felt drawn towards reading the book solely because of the concept. There are people living in the world or supposedly dead, or reborn or whatever in some explosion in the 80’s. They are now reunited in the present world. Two of them are latent and the girl is a human. 

Story line: story-line, yes. Okay wait. Is there a story? Oh yeah... the boy finds the girl. Villain enters. Kills boy and girl. Story over. Sorry... spoiler!! 


Language: language is very flat. It is devoid of any emotion. There are many unnecessary details such as the paperback on the table near the hospital bed, the dining chairs etc etc. 

Good points: the book started well. First two chapters are very interesting. The concept of course is very intriguing. It raises your expectations from the book. 

Not-so-good points: there are so many elements that I wished were there in the book. Such as a story. Strong characters. A good language trail. So on and so forth. But it disappointed me throughout. This is it. I am not touching another fantasy novel for a long time.

Overall: i wish I had never come across the book. But now that I have and I have read it, I would ask those who want to read it to not have any expectations. One can probably teach English with the help of this book since there were no spelling or grammatical mistakes.
