ebook, First, 11 pages
Published October 31st 2013 by www.devinberglund.com

This book is a fable. It is about losing hope and gaining it again while you cannot seem to move on in life, when there is not driving force in life. I received this book as a review copy in exchange for an unbiased review on amazon.com.  I am glad I came across this book. A well written fable which makes you appreciate your life. Here is how I rate the book. 

Cover-page: the cover-page is well designed but it hardly talks about  the content of the book. I would have liked it to be more descriptive about the content of the book. But this works well too. There are leaves and there is a girl who looks like she is stoned, no pun intended!!

Characters: the main character in the book is called silvia. There is a girl named ember who helps silvia through her painful life. Characters are very strong and interesting. That is it!!

Concept: a woman named silvia is a widow who loses her husband to a car accident. She almost gives up on her life. The story is about resurrecting hope. Interesting concept. This is the reason I picked up this book. Now I remember… :P  

Story line: the lead character, Silvia, loses her husband to a car accident. There is no one in her life to give meaning to it. She survives it because of the tales told to her b
 Her mother about the treasures in life. She lives life without meaning till she meets ember, a girl who gets lost in the woods. She tries to help ember but ember leaves giving her a gift of life time. A very interesting fable for those who like reading about mythical characters.

Language: the language is simple and very gripping. The author has a good command over stirring emotions.

Good points: the book is written in a very simple manner. The concentration is over the story rather than the writing. It is also very short and leaves a wonderful message behind.

Not-so-good points: well, I wished the book be a little longer. It finishes way too quickly!!

Overall: the book is an interesting read for those who like reading about fables and myth. It is a good attempt on author’s behalf but may not click with audience who have been fans of Paulo Coelho and Mitch Albom.

Give It a shot but keep your expectations low!!
