Ryan is one of the author’s that I stalk! Literally! I always look forward to reading her books and for that reason, bug her till she tells me when her next book is releasing! Yeah I am that crazy! So, Ryan has been kind this time around too and let me have an ARC copy of her next book (and final in the series). It’s called PANIC. I am so excited to post this review! A) Because it is the best ending I have come across to the series and, b) Ryan has done a great job on this one as well (and I have come to expect nothing less from her). So here is my review!


Cover-page:  well, it’s better than earlier books in the series but I am not swept off my feet! It could have been definitely better. I would say somehow it has failed to reveal much about the content again. But what lacks in the cover is fulfilled by the writing itself. So no complains!

Characters:  The book reunites kent and robin who had distanced themselves from each other in the first book. There are also two new characters in the book called bailey and shayna. Ryan has a special talent of designing her characters and giving them depth. There are minor details in the book which bring you closer to the characters and understand them. For example when Ryan writes about bailey, ““no, thanks”, I say, but my stupid dick betrays me, getting hard from the whore’s touch.” Ryan completely gets into bailey’s character and writes exactly how a guy like him would talk! He also in denial when he somewhere knows “I was her first that night. Still, I can’t hold back from saying more hurtful things her words begin to sink in, because something like this just can’t be true.” This would be the impulsive reaction of anyone who panics when he learns the truth. And Ryan does not try to create heroes. She just describes the ounce of hero that resides in every devil, beautifully. This is what I have come to love about her writing. Shayna of course is a girl who comes to the big city with her dreams but gets into a not so beautiful situation. She is also a lovable character in the book but my favorite remains bailey, hands down!

 "No one gives away the cheesier slice unless there's an ulterior motive" - Shayna, PANIC 

Story line: the book follows the characters of the first book in the series "FAST". Kent and Robin went different ways in the first book in the series and the author brings them back in this book. Kent realizes that Robin was hiding a huge secret from him and therefore the distancing act. But now he cannot stay away from her. Robin on the other hand knows that she cannot give Kent the life he has always wanted but somewhere in her heart she cannot extinguish the love for him. Bailey is “the kid” in his local friends group. But he hardly thinks so. Shayna is a small town girl who comes to the city to find the purpose. She meets bailey and what happens next is the basis of their entire story. Ryan connects Kent and Robin’s story to Bailey and Shayna’s and it’s a job wonderfully done.

Concept: concept is just amazing! I loved the way the story turns around and finally everything falls in place. In spite of there being bitterness somewhere.

Language: Ryan writes in a very simple language but she writes emotions beautifully. There are going to be paragraphs that are going to make you cry. Have a pack of tissues beside when you read the book!

Good points: I loved the concept. The characterization is deep and thoughtful. The story is twisted and has a happy ending! Loved it.  

Bad points: the cover could have been much better. It does not do justice to the book at all.



Overall: what do I say! The series could not have had a better ending. I loved everything about the book. This is a  must read for all the romance lovers. Truly!
