There are some books that you are meant to read. This is one of those books. I picked this one up randomly from story cartel and stared reading. I never knew it is going to leave a mark on me this way. This is definitely one of the best books I have read in this year. Let me explain how.


Cover-page:  even though the cover does not look extraordinary, it does leave an impression on you. The designer has definitely succeeded in leaving a mark. On the cover what looks like are the two main characters of the book. The cover-page is well composed and not over done. Well done.

Characters:  The book is about Jolie. She is a girl who tops in everything in her class. She is a loyal friend. She is especially good with literature and playing violin. Sam ‘Romeo’ Romero is the love interest of Jolie. Sam is the boy next door with ultimate good looks and possesses a power of speech which will leave anyone impressed.

Both the characters are sketched out very well. The narration is in first person. Therefore it brings you closer to Jolie. I like her character more because I feel she resembles me in some ways. The quiet girl in the class who is always engrossed in books and has a major complex. Jolie has me written all over her!

Sam beats his script against his leg and the pages flutter. "You and Jeremy Spinner."

I don't nod. I don't shake my head. I stand there, the first traces of donkey teeth making themselves known beneath my upper lip. Only then do I realize that I've hardly felt them all day - until now.



Story line: the book is about Jolie and how she overcomes her fear of speech. Jolie earns straight A’s in all her subjects except speech. She is about to fail but one good at heart teacher recommends her for the speech team which could earn her extra credit. But she still doesn’t know which will she prefer. Failing or speaking in front of a crowd. Thus enters the hero of the book. Sam offers her to coach her for the prose. The only problem is that he is from the rival school team and is not allowed to do so.

The book is an interesting journey of Jolie and how she learns to speak in front of a crowd.







Concept: the concept is very interesting. I loved the fact that someone could think about such a concept and write a whole book over it. Just brilliant!

Language: the author writes in first person. I usually do not like reading a book which keeps saying I, me and myself in every stanza but this book just grows on you. The language is innocent. It is from a POV of a teenager and how passing speech is the most important thing in her life. The book also has a classic type of feel to it. it is like reading a novel who’s story has taken place long time back. Like reading a memoir! I may have finally come across an author who has her own style of writing and will not get overshadowed by popcorn books.

To thine own self be true.


Good points: the characters in the book are drawn beautifully. the concept is refreshingly different. I loved the language too. It has a very classic feel to it.

Bad points: the storyline is not bad but I believe it could have been better. The author has tried to pull in boyfriend strings and best friend quarrel in between but it does not weave into an engaging story. These points could have been played better.

Overall: I loved it. Truly. I believe everyone who is a book lover and relates to Jane Eyre is going to love this book. There is another book by the same author which has caught my attention. Would definitely read that and get back with a review. While I do that be sure to read this one! It’s great!
