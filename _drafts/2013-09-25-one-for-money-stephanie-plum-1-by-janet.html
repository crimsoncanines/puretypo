Kindle Edition, 352 pages
Published (first published 1994)

This is my second book by janet. When i read the first book which happened to be the tenth in the stephenie plum series I was blown out of my mind. So i decided to read the entire series from the very beginning.

One for the money is the first book in the series about the bounty hunter stephenie plum. The lead character stephnie plum is a broke girl living in her own flat. She has been selling her furniture to make the ends meet. And finally there is time when she decides that she needs a job which pays her enormously and takes care of all her expenses. With a lot pushing and blackmailing, Vinnie, her cousin gives her the job to arrest j\Joe morelli, a police officer on a run.

Stephnie tries and fails again and again to bring him under. Joe morelli is accused of murder of an unarmed man. Its also Stephnie's personal mission to bring him in to settle some previous scores.

During the adventure stephnie realizes that she is dealing with a professional. There are lots of hilarious moments. Grandma mazur is my favourite and worth watching out for. The tactful Joe morelli comes across as a bad guy in the beginning but ends up winning hearts. You are going to hate yourself for loving him. 

There is a lot of suspense and thrill as well. The book in all is a very good read. It is going to leave you rolling on the floor laughing!! Janet is slowly climbing up to be my favorite comic writer. A must read from her series.

4 stars

cheers!!



MADE INTO MOVIE!!




One for the money is a humorous book and a bang opposite of a movie!! The movie stars Katherine Heigl as Stephenie Plum and Jason O'Mara as Joe Morelli. The actors are good at their job but somehow fail to impress. Movie lacks humor. Or I should say it is very plain. Ones who have read the book prior are going to be very disappointed. But i guess making a Stephenie Plum movie is not a child's play, is it? 

For those who have read the book - Movie is not half as interesting as the book! Do not watch the movie. You are going to hate yourself for wasting your precious time. Which you could use to, I don't know, wash a dish maybe!

For those who have not read the book - Read the above piece of advice. The book is not half as interesting as the book. It is better to get hold of the book and start reading than watch the movie. The book is not for the book eating bookworms but a casual reading is what it requires. And moreover taking a shortcut by watching instead of reading is only going to leave you scarred!! So... For the sake of good humor, READ THE BOOK!
