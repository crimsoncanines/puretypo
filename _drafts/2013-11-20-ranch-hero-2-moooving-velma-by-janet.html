Paperback, 38 pages
Published May 2013 by thewordverve inc.

Disclosure: I received this copy in exchange for an honest review for amazon.com

I guess I am in my “art-y” phase now. i am going through a number of children’s book and studying illustrations that are done in the book. Writing a child’s book is definitely not a child’s play. And a lot of people are not getting it right. Again this book is there but not really there. Here is what I think about the book.


Cover-page:  cover-page is a very interesting. The illustration is done very well here. it does a pretty good job of attracting kids’ attention.

Characters: characters are designed pretty well. There is a family of dogs. The “super-dog” is designed with all the super powers which will keep the children entertained.

Concept: the entire concept of the book is very interesting. Writing a story about dogs can be quite challenging. But here it flows very well.

Story line: story is about a dog which is the hero of the ranch. There are two donkeys in the story who are the negative characters. Story seems gripping.  

Language/illustration: ah. Now is the sad part. Where the story is pretty interesting the writing is just flat. I wish it was written with a bit of fun element. The illustrations are also very boring and “in a box”. The composition may not appeal many.

Good points: the good points are definitely the story and the cover page. This will definitely help in the sale. Kids will enjoy it too.

Bad points: remember how we got hooked to cinderealla and snowshite when we were kids? I am still looking for a book which matches up to those. I may be keeping my expectations high but….

Overall: overall the book is interesting but does not really hold somebody’s attention for long. It could have been definitely better illustrated.  But yeah. Kids will love it no matter what. Especially the colors and the “super-dog”.
