I have been trying to read books from different genres and in this unquenchable thirst of finding the next best book for me! This is one such book that I would love my friends to read and recommend their friends too. Found this one on story cartel in exchange for an honest review. This is a book by an incest victim finally gathering the courage to come out in open and talk about it. I am sure this book is going to help many other to shed their fear and shame and talk about it. here is what I think of the book itself.

Cover-page:  cover-page depicts a faceless girl. I appreciate the thought process behind the character but it may not appeal to many readers. It lacks the eye catching factor. It does not give a feeling of being a memoir too. Needless to say a better approach could have been taken to design the cover.

 "our lives being to end the day we become silent about things that matter. " - MARTIN LUTHER KING


Characters: the lead character in the book is the author itself. She narrates her story of forcefully being in a incestuous relationship with her father while her mother was not knowing anything about it for 12 years. The lead character leaves a very strong impact on the reader.    

Story line: the book covers the three periods of her life. Childhood, adolescent and adult. The author describes how she was forced to satisfy her father in every which way. The author decides to hold no bars and tells the story in all its raw form because “that’s the way it happened and that’s how it must be told”. in the book she is often breaking down, fearing for his father’s rage, cursing herself for being at fault and always in want of help from her mother who never sees what happens in her own house or debated by the author, sees it but doesn’t do anything about it. The book also contains a collection of poems she wrote which describe her feelings at the given time.

Concept: the concept is not new but it is a book different by itself. The concept of child abuse has been used in many books but this one still breaks your heart. Especially because it comes from the victim itself.

Language: the book is written in a very simple language. The author is not intending to set any literary benchmark but just to reach the readers who are in need of support. The language is straight forward and without any swirls. There are few sentences that are often repeating in the book. but overall it is a simple read.

Good points: author’s narration is commendable. There are going to be times when you are going to loathe the father for being such an animal to her own daughter. It is a well written piece for sure.   

Bad points: the bad point about the book would definitely be the cover. I wish the cover was done a little more thoughtfully so the readers would not be able to give it a pass. The faceless girl on a sill just doesn’t work.   

Overall: overall the book is a good but depressing read. Not everybody would be able to take it in. it is definitely not for the weak hearts. On the other hand it is going to help the victims and the families of victims to recover from the trauma. The author also encourages the victims to get in touch with her and talk it out. I will recommend this book to as many people as I can so they are more aware and are able to protect their loved ones from such a misery.
