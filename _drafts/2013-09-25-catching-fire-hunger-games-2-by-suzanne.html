Hardcover, First Edition, 391 pages
Published September 1st 2009 by Scholastic Press

This is the second book in the hunger games trilogy. After reading the first book i could not contain myself and went on to read the second book. there was a sense on curiosity,a will to know what happens nest which get completely washed away towards the fist half of the book.

The book is has a good beginning, a so-so mid and a pathetic end. The female lead, Katnis comes back to her district after winning the games with her fellow tribute Peeta. Here Peeta realises that Katnis was only pretending to be in love with him to save both of them in games. He goes on to feel bad to feeling pathetic and then to being just friends with her. On the other hand all the districts in Panem are up for a rebellion which Katnis has supposedly instigated. Now Capitol insists her to marry Peeta or face dire consequences. And just when you would think she is back to her district and would live happily ever after, she is thrown back to the arena to fight till death. With Peeta.

The whole story goes on to be little unrealistic when a seventeen years old girl is forced into arena, decked up to get married and declared pregnant. You might find yourself getting disconnected with the track. 

The writing style is good as ever. Only disappointment is the course of the story. After seeing a lot happening in the first book the second book comes across as a disappointment. My suggestion, read the book but just to know the further story. Do not have too many expectations.
