I received this book in exchange of an honest review from net galley.

Cozy classics is a series of classic literature which is converted into a book board. These books are illustrated versions of the classics which depict the prime characters in wool dolls. It is interesting to see that the book consists of only a few words which may mean something to the child when he learns about the novels. These books can be a huge help for parents to introduce the classics to their child and maybe ask them to write a summary of the books. I am thankful for net galley to give me an opportunity to read and review these books. They were fun!

Cover-page:  cover-page depicts the dolls which form the main characters of the book. they are sweet and appealing to the young crowd. Kudos!!

Characters: the characters are the main lead and few important characters of the original series. They are made out fabric and portrayed keeping in mind their important characteristics. Well turned out.

Story line: there is not much of a story here. it consists of few words which may mean something to someone who has read the series himself earlier. They are focused on kids to interpret. Well done.

Concept: the whole concept is very interesting. This book can be used to teach kids a thing or two about literature. The concept may be appealing to some but may also be rejected as it feels very incomplete.

Language: language is very simple. The words are very simple and short. Easy to learn and grasp.

Good points: the concept of the book is very interesting. The fact that there are blank pages here and there makes it easier to be used in different ways. The characters are well identified and portrayed.

Bad points: however basics it is, I would still like to see a word or two about the characters. A child who has not read the classics or has not been told about it may find it difficult to relate to. The words may come out to be very vague.

Overall: overall I feel the book is a nice attempt to teach kids about classics. I would love to see more books coming out with similar concept. It is refreshingly original. Would recommend with four stars.
