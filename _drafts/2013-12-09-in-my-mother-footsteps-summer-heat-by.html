Published July 2nd 2013 by Renaissance Romance Publishing

I received this book in exchange for an honest review on Amazon. This book is a travel book. Itâ€™s a journey of a woman who realizes she has failed to understand her mom that she needs to follow her footsteps to really know her mother better. So she sets off on a journey. The book is a good attempt to write a story as it happens to one person, but somewhere it fails to connect with the reader. Here is how I feel about the book. 

Cover-page: the cover-page is very simple. It looks like a philosophical book by the cover but it is not. This somehow does not go well with me. But it is not bad either. Works well but fails to impress.

Characters: there is Anela who is upset with her mother for not telling her that her father exists. But she cannot stay that way for long since. Her mother leaves her a house a letter and a ticket to her favorite place on earth. Anela takes the ticket and leaves the town on this amazing journey. And also to find her father. 

Concept: the concept is nothing great but could be woven into a great story. One must have come across many books about finding the father etc and this one is no different.

Story line: it is pretty predictable except everything turns out to be well and good in the end. That was not very expected. 



Language: I am not a big fan of the writing either. It is very flat and devoid of any emotions. I still do not understand the smirk and crooked smile!! Very boring. 


Overall: overall the book is a good light read for the people who like to read travel and how it changes people's lives. But other than that the book is not the wonder of the world. It is simple. One will read it and feel good and move on to other books.
