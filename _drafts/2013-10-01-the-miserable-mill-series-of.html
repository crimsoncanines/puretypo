Paperback, 194 pages
Published 2001 by Scholastic (first published January 1st 2000)

Ok... so I should say I am getting bored now. This book is no different from the earlier ones. But just too boring of a plot. The kids are sent to another care taker who apparently has no name. Everybody calls him "SIR". Also he owns a lumber mill and illegally makes people work for him. He is way too filmy...!! Doesnâ€™t have a face. People just see the smoke from his cigar instead of his face. Ah...!! I would prefer Aunt Josephine coming back to life here. SIR is intolerable. 

So, he is the new care taker of these kids and he makes them work in his mill. Which is cruel I know. But what is crueler is that he feeds them gum for lunch!! And he employs Dr Ornwell who is the hypnotist. ooopppss... the ophthalmologist. He employs a receptionist and together they conspire to snatch away the family fortune of the kids. Ah!! I am tired already. There are few more characters. But its better you read about them!! Why should I be the only one subjected to the misery!! 

The author has tried role reversal here. Violet tries to read a heavy book and Klaus tries to invent. The book is really interesting in the beginning because the kids are in different setting altogether and there is a sense of curiosity. But it just gets boring from the middle of the book. There was a lot of scope for writing a good story but I m really disappointed. And SOMEBODY PLEASE HELP Mr. POE CATCH COUNT OLAF AND PLEASE LETS BE DONE WITH THIS MAN!!! I am so done with him escaping at the end of every book.

There were few good things about the book though. The writing style is as always very pleasing, for a young adult as well as the oldies. There is a bit of humor also at few places. But all these things could not be a substitute for a boring plot. I would give it 2.5 stars and I am not losing hope yet!! Will definitely carry on with the other books in the series.
